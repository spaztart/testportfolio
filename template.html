A Bit of History:

McAfee is a global leader in online protection, serving over 100 million devices worldwide. In 2020, McAfee underwent rapid organizational changes, with shifts in leadership and evolving company objectives. Teams across marketing, design, and engineering worked in silos, pushing products out quickly to meet tight deadlines.

This led to visual inconsistencies in our products and varying UX patterns. A few key issues emerged:

  • Different developer frameworks were used across various products.

  • Designers often overrode components with their own styles to match their vision for the products.
  • Documentation and guidelines were lacking.

Given the state of the current design processes, we had to address these challenges by building a design system that could bring scalability, efficiency, and brand consistency for McAfee‚Äôs products. So how did we accomplish this?

New Beginnings:

I joined the design system team in 2021 to take part in leading the design efforts. As a result, I came up with a phased approach:

Gain Understanding:

I started by meeting with the desktop, mobile, brand, and marketing teams. From here, I analyzed the prime UI libraries they use for components, icons, and visual foundations. I also learned about the front-end frameworks engineers use. This helped me define goals and determine success metrics.

Audit Process:

Collaborating with other design teams, I started auditing typefaces, colors, and UI components throughout their design flows, identifying commonly used UI components, and coming up with a solidified library for McAfee's main base components.

Time Constraints:

The rapid release of product features created multiple parallel projects for designers and engineers, making it challenging to balance workloads while building a design system.

Adoption:

Some engineering teams resisted adopting the new design system due to bandwidth constraints.

Leaked UI Libraries:

Designers began using new UI components in the design process, leading to a mix of old and new components being used. This influenced engineers to build their own UI for these components.

Technical Challenges:

Integrating the design system into existing workflows and technologies required careful planning to ensure compatibility with different platforms, frameworks, and tools used by various teams.

Lack of Governance:

Designers would bring us stakeholder-approved UI components to build because their product engineers didn't have time to create them. Once the components were built, many were rarely reused by engineers and designers.
